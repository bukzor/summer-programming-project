For a kid coming from making a game, motivation depends on tight
cause-and-effect visual feedback: change a line, immediately see a sprite
move, a color change, a score update. Tools that route through a
print-to-console loop (plain Python scripts, no graphics) tend to lose kids
in this age range within a few sessions, regardless of how "beginner
friendly" the syntax is, because there's nothing to show for the work.
