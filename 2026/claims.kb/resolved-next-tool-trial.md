Not a unilateral switch to a new tool. A bounded trial: 1-2 sessions in
Python (currently pyxel — `../deductions.kb/why-pyxel.md`), then an
explicit, named checkpoint conversation where Abby gets a real vote —
continue in Python, or go back to full-time Scratch — with no penalty
either way. Scratch stays available to her on her own time regardless of
what the summer-session tool ends up being.

**Placement (user, 2026-07-11):** the checkpoint sits at the end of
session 1 (`../curriculum.kb/design/060-deliverables.kb/session-01-blocks.md`),
counting session 0 (planning + window) as the first of the "1-2 sessions"
and session 1 (blocks — first world that visibly "reads as Minecraft") as
the second: the earliest point there's a real payoff to weigh against
Scratch. Wired into
`../curriculum.kb/design/060-deliverables.kb/session-facilitation-protocol.md`
so it isn't optional. This resolves
`../claims.kb/trial-execution-lacks-exit-ramp.md`.
