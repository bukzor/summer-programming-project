Abby's concrete example of an "intense thing": a full copy of an existing
game, specifically citing Minecraft. There are two independent reasons
this isn't achievable as stated, and the first response to her conflated
them (Buck caught this — see below):

1. **Capability ceiling** (tool-specific, timeframe-independent): the
   actual "Minecraft in Scratch" projects that exist are simplified 2D
   tributes, not full recreations (`scratch-minecraft-clones-are-simplified-tributes.md`).
   Scratch structurally can't reach full-Minecraft scope even given
   unlimited time.
2. **Project scope** (tool-independent, timeframe-specific): even in a
   general-purpose language, a full commercial-sandbox-game clone is a
   multi-year effort — not achievable in an 8-week summer project in any
   tool.

Neither is a reason to override her preference on tools; it's a signal
that session zero's "her game, her call" step needs to reframe the
ambition, not shrink it: pick the one piece of Minecraft she actually
cares about (placing/breaking blocks? a world to walk around in?
crafting? a specific enemy?) and scope *that* small enough to hit an
early win.
