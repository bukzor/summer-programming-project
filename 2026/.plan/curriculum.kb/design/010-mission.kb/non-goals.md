Not a goal: a polished or shippable game, software-engineering best practice
(classes, tests, type hints), or covering "all of Python." The standard is
kid-readable and kid-authored, not production-grade — that's why
`../../technical-policy.kb/single-file-no-abstractions.md` forbids the very
patterns a professional codebase would want.

Also explicitly out of the core arc (not out of the project — see
`../070-future-work.kb/`): player physics/collision, a scrolling camera,
drag-and-drop UI, shaped recipes. Each is the hardest engineering in any
Minecraft-like, with near-zero beginner-Python payoff.
