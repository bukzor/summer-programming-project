Every session plants one bug (or lets a real one happen) on purpose: an
off-by-one, a swapped `row`/`col`, a missing subtraction, a bad gate. This
turns "errors are detective work" from a stated value into a rehearsed
skill, and gives predict-before-run a guaranteed wrong-prediction moment
instead of relying on chance. The best ones double as game lore ("is that a
bug or a cheat code?").
