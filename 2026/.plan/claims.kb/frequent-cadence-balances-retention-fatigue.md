Every 2-3 days is the floor, not a ceiling: frequent enough that she
doesn't forget where things left off between sessions (a real risk with
syntax-based debugging, unlike Scratch's blocks which are
self-documenting). Daily is fine, even better for retention, if that's
what schedules actually allow and she's still into it — the risk on that
end isn't retention, it's "it's summer, I want unstructured time too,"
which is a fatigue/burnout question, not a memory one. Read the room
session to session rather than defending a fixed number either direction.

**Update (user, 2026-07-11):** daily is the aspiration, every 2-3 days is
the realistic floor given actual scheduling — see
`../claims.kb/recommended-cadence.md`.
