Checked directly (web search) rather than assumed: "Minecraft in Scratch"
projects exist and are a known genre of Scratch tutorial/community
project, but they are simplified 2D tributes — basic block placement and
inventory tracking via variables/broadcasts, sometimes a simple crafting
mechanic — not full recreations. Tutorials for them explicitly distinguish
themselves as "2D, not 3D." No evidence of a feature-complete Minecraft
clone in Scratch. This means the ceiling claim itself ("Scratch is capable
of a full Minecraft") doesn't hold even given unlimited time — Scratch's
single-threaded, interpreted, no-real-3D-pipeline execution model is a
structural ceiling, not just a matter of nobody having gotten around to
it. Separate from, and in addition to, the 8-week-summer scope argument in
`abby-goal-full-game-clone.md`.
