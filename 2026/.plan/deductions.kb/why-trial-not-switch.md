Her preference for Scratch is real and not based on missing information —
we tested the two concrete downsides and neither moved her
(scratch-friction-points-dont-move-her). Her consent to try Python was
reluctant, not enthusiastic (abby-reluctant-trial-consent). Stacking a
reluctant kid onto a tool she didn't choose, with no exit, is how a
project quietly dies in week 3 — so the honest design isn't "switch her,"
it's "bound the switch to a trial with a real checkpoint," which only
works if that first trial session actually delivers the early win
(kids-need-early-win) fast enough to give her something to weigh against
her existing Scratch fluency.
